Geography of Bern

Short description of Bern

An overview of Bern’s location and climate

Location

Bern is located on the Swiss Plateau, in the canton of Bern, slightly west of the country’s center and about 20 km north of the Bernese Alps.

Landscape formation

The surrounding area was shaped by glaciers during the last Ice Age.

Surrounding hills

Two small mountains shape the surrounding landscape:

  1. Gurten (858 meters)
  2. Bantiger (947 meters)

Urban topography

The city initially developed on a peninsula formed by a meander of the Aare River, which limited its natural expansion until the 19th century, when bridges allowed expansion beyond the river.

Altitude differences

Bern’s urban terrain is quite irregular: there are differences of several dozen meters between neighborhoods near the river and those at higher elevations.

Area and land use

Bern covers a total area of 51.6 km², distributed as follows:

  • 20.2% agricultural land
  • 33.5% forests
  • 44.2% urban areas (buildings and roads)
  • 2.1% unproductive (rivers or glaciers)

Urban topography

The city developed on a peninsula formed by the Aare River.

Altitude differences

Bern’s terrain shows clear differences between low-lying and higher neighborhoods.

Climate

Bern has an oceanic climate (Köppen Cfb).
